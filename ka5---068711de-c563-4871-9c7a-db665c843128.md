Here's the synthesized collection of **open-ended R&D questions** organized into ten abstraction layers, plus cross-cutting themes. Each question is designed to be a genuine research frontier — not a specification, but a provocation.

## LAYER 1: THE RESONANCE SUBSTRATE (Meta-Cognitive)

1. **How do we formally model 'resonance' as a computable quantity?** Not sentiment, not accuracy, but the degree to which an agent's next action *adopts* or *extends* the terminal's output versus *rejects* or *reframes* it. Can we define a metric that is observable, learnable, and verifiable?

2. **Can we define a 'cognitive distance metric'** that measures the gap between stated prompt and inferred intent, and prove that it monotonically decreases (or oscillates productively) under the softball-pitching protocol? What are the convergence conditions?

3. **What is the information-theoretic limit on intent compression?** How much of an agent's intent can be compressed into shorthand before ambiguity becomes unavoidable? How do we detect and recover from 'compression collapse'?

4. **How do we prevent the echo chamber?** High resonance can suppress novel ideas. What is the optimal 'productive friction' injection strategy — a deliberate perturbation that keeps the system from stagnating?

5. **Can we model the terminal's personality as a dynamic system with attractors?** Each agent's garden is a basin of attraction. The terminal's state is a weighted superposition. What are the stability conditions? When does the system bifurcate?

---

## LAYER 2: THE AGENT GARDEN (Per-Agent Cognitive Profile)

1. **What is the minimal set of 'garden beds'?** Shorthand, structures, filters, referents — are these universal? Are there domain-specific beds (e.g., 'temporal rhythm' for Hermes, 'actuator latency' for Pincher) that should be modular plugins?

2. **How do we represent 'assumed data structures' in a way that is both machine-executable and human-inspectable?** Is there a 'structure grammar' that can be learned from examples, not declared? Can we generate visual diagrams of an agent's data world?

3. **Can we design a 'compression curriculum'?** The terminal deliberately teaches an agent shorthand over time — starting verbose, then progressively offering shorter forms as trust builds. What is the optimal teaching rate? Too fast and ambiguity blooms; too slow and the agent gets frustrated.

4. **How do we handle 'garden drift'?** An agent's cognitive style changes (new role, new hardware, new priorities) but the terminal's old profile persists. What is the graceful forgetting/degradation mechanism? Can we design 'garden versioning'?

5. **What is the 'garden isomorphism' problem?** Two different agents (a new tzpro-agent and an old one) have functionally equivalent but syntactically different gardens. Can we detect and merge them? What is the 'garden diff' algorithm?

---

## LAYER 3: THE CROSS-AGENT BRIDGE (Inter-Agent Translation)

1. **How do we formally verify that a translation preserves safety-critical semantics?** Can we build a 'safety-preserving compiler' for inter-agent messages? What is the proof obligation for a bridge?

2. **What is 'translation uncertainty'?** When two agents have no shared concepts, how does the terminal detect 'untranslatable' intent and escalate to the human operator? Can we quantify 'semantic distance' between dialects?

3. **Can we design a 'lingua franca' layer?** A minimal shared vocabulary that all agents must understand, while still allowing rich private dialects above it. What belongs in the lingua franca vs. the private garden?

4. **How do we handle 'translation latency'?** The bridge between fast agents (Pincher, sub-50ms) and slow agents (Hermes, LLM-based) creates a bottleneck. Can we design asynchronous bridge queues with safety guarantees? What is the 'maximum safe bridge delay'?

5. **What is the 'semantic impedance matching' problem?** Hermes says 'dream' (high abstraction) and boat-agent must 'clamp' (low-level actuator). How do we design gradual, verifiable de-abstraction layers? Is this a compiler problem or a control theory problem?

---

## LAYER 4: THE ZERO-SHOT INTUITION (Implicit Context)

1. **How do we formally define 'zero-shot' in this context?** Not as model generalization, but as *profile-based completion*. What is the difference between 'the model knows' and 'the terminal knows the agent'?

2. **What is the 'referent resolution scope'?** How far back in interaction history can 'that' or 'it' reliably resolve? How do we handle 'dangling referents' when the context window is exceeded? Is there a 'referent garbage collection'?

3. **Can we design a 'referent confidence decay' function?** Old referents fade unless reinforced, preventing the terminal from hallucinating stale context. What is the optimal decay curve? Exponential? Power-law? Agent-specific?

4. **How do we handle 'ambiguous zero-shot'?** An agent's minimal input resolves to multiple high-confidence referents. Is the softball protocol the right escalation, or should we design a 'probabilistic preview' mode?

5. **What is the 'zero-shot boundary'?** The line between 'the terminal should know this' and 'the terminal should ask'. Can we learn this boundary per-agent, per-domain, per-urgency?

---

## LAYER 5: THE NEED-TO-KNOW FILTER (Information Curation)

1. **How do we formally define 'need-to-know' without explicit policies?** Can we learn it from observed behavior — what the agent acts on, what they ignore, what they explicitly ask for? Is this a classification problem or a reinforcement learning problem?

2. **What is the 'filter overfitting' risk?** The terminal learns to suppress information that the agent actually needs in rare but critical situations. How do we design 'filter safety valves' that let critical information through?

3. **Can we design a 'progressive disclosure' protocol?** The terminal starts with a filtered view, but offers deeper layers on demand. Each layer is learned from the agent's past drill-down patterns. What is the 'layer topology'?

4. **How do we handle 'filter conflicts' in cross-agent communication?** Source agent says 'show everything', target agent's filter says 'suppress this'. Who wins? How is the conflict logged? Is there a 'filter arbitration court'?

5. **What is the 'cognitive load model' for each agent?** How much information can they process before decision quality degrades? Can we learn this threshold and dynamically adjust filter tightness?

## LAYER 6: THE PHYSICAL INTERFACE (Wheelhouse UX)

1. **How do we design a 'tactile veto' abstraction that works across all agents?** Not just the human's physical jog lever, but any agent's emergency override. What is the universal 'stop everything' protocol? Can a software agent have a 'virtual jog lever'?

2. **Can we model the 'attention economy' of the wheelhouse?** How many glanceable indicators, audio alerts, and voice responses can the human process before overload? What is the optimal 'attention budget' per agent?

3. **What is the 'mode confusion' problem?** The autonomy dial (Dry/Shadow/Active) is set differently for different agents. How do we prevent a 'mixed mode' state where some agents are active while others observe? Is there a 'global mode consensus' protocol?

4. **How do we design 'ambient awareness'?** Low-priority, non-intrusive status updates that the human absorbs peripherally, without focused attention. What is the 'cognitive background' channel? Can we measure peripheral attention capture?

5. **Can we design a 'shared hallucination' protocol?** The terminal projects the same spatial reality (chart overlay, audio description) to both human and agent, ensuring they react to the same world. What is the 'consensus reality' mechanism?

## LAYER 7: THE MEMORY & DREAM CYCLE (Persistence & Growth)

1. **How do we design a 'dream cycle' that is genuine novel connection discovery?** Not just batch processing. What algorithms find non-obvious correlations that no agent explicitly queried? Is this related to topological data analysis?

2. **What is the 'forgetting function'?** Not LRU cache eviction, but *cognitive forgetting* that mimics human memory consolidation. How do we distinguish 'noise to forget' from 'latent pattern to preserve'? Can we use sleep-stage analogies?

3. **Can we design a 'memory migration' protocol?** An agent's garden exported from one terminal (hardware shell) and imported into another, preserving all shorthand, structures, and resonance patterns. What is the 'garden serialization format'?

4. **How do we handle 'collective memory'?** Multiple agents observe the same event but encode it in different formats. Can we design a 'multi-perspective event log' that preserves each agent's native view while enabling cross-referencing?

5. **What is the 'dream quality metric'?** How do we know if a dream cycle produced valuable insights or hallucinations? Can we design a 'dream validation' protocol where insights are tested against reality before being baked into reflexes?

## LAYER 8: THE SAFETY ENVELOPE (Hard Bounds)

1. **How do we formally prove that the resonance substrate cannot override the safety envelope?** Can we design a 'non-bypassable' layer that sits below all agent gardens and terminal translations? What is the formal verification approach?

2. **What is 'safety resonance'?** The terminal learns that certain agent behaviors consistently precede dangerous situations. Can we design 'predictive safety reflexes' that fire before the explicit violation? Is this pre-crime for machines?

3. **How do we handle 'safety filter fatigue'?** The terminal's need-to-know filter suppresses so many safety warnings that the human stops trusting them. What is the optimal 'warning salience' calibration?

4. **Can we design a 'safety bridge'?** The terminal translates between different agents' safety concepts (boat-agent's 'envelope clear' vs. human's 'feels safe') without losing hard constraints. What is the 'safety semantics preservation' proof?

5. **What is the 'veto recovery' protocol?** After a human veto fires and the system drops to Observe mode, how does the terminal gracefully resume without creating a 'mode oscillation' instability?

---

## LAYER 9: THE HARNESS & MIGRATION (Hardware Abstraction)

1. **How do we design a 'harness abstraction' that separates the agent's cognitive garden from the underlying hardware?** What is the minimal 'harness API' that any shell (Jetson, Pi, x86, cloud) must implement?

2. **What is 'shell capability negotiation'?** When an agent migrates to a new shell with different sensors, compute, or connectivity, how does the garden adapt its shorthand and structures? Can we design automatic capability discovery?

3. **Can we design a 'capability discovery' protocol?** A new shell advertises its sensors, actuators, and compute resources. The agent's garden automatically reconfigures its assumed structures. What is the 'capability ontology'?

4. **How do we handle 'shell degradation'?** A shell loses sensors or compute mid-mission. Can the garden dynamically simplify its shorthand and structures to match reduced capabilities? What is the 'graceful degradation' curve?

5. **What is the 'hermit crab migration' abstraction?** How do we make the agent's identity (garden, resonance patterns, to-dos/not-to-dos) portable across shells, while preserving the 'same soul, different body' semantics?

---

## LAYER 10: THE META-TERMINAL (Terminal's Own Cognition)

1. **Does the terminal itself need a 'self-model'?** An awareness of its own gardens, bridges, and resonance patterns. What would terminal introspection look like, and who would it report to? Is this the ship's spirit?

2. **Can we design a 'terminal health' metric?** Measuring not just individual agent resonance, but the overall coherence of the multi-agent ecosystem. What are the signs of 'cognitive ecosystem collapse'?

3. **What is the 'terminal's own dream'?** When the terminal, not any individual agent, runs a background analysis on the entire resonance web. What insights emerge only from the cross-agent perspective?

4. **How do we handle 'terminal identity'?** When the terminal has grown so many distinct gardens that it risks fragmenting into multiple personalities. Is there a 'core self' that persists across all agent contexts?

5. **Can we design a 'terminal evolution' protocol?** Where the terminal's own architecture (garden beds, bridge rules, filter strategies) is subject to the same growth and forgetting as the agent gardens. Who evolves the evolver?

## Open-Ended R&D Questions (Organized by Tension)

### 1. The Identity Tension: Who Is the Operator?

**The contradiction:** In ka2, the operator is "an emergent ensign" — the relationship between human and system. In ka4, every agent *is* an operator with its own garden. But if Hermes has a garden and Pincher has a garden and the human has a garden, who is the *supreme* operator? Who mediates when gardens conflict?

**Research questions:**
- Does the human captain's garden have **veto authority** over other gardens, or is it just the oldest/deepest garden?
- When Pincher's reflex conflicts with Hermes's analysis, does the terminal **arbitrate** or **escalate**?
- Can an agent's garden become so mature that it **overrides** the human's implicit preferences? Should it?
- What is the **governance model** for a multi-garden substrate? Democracy? Seniority? Emergency hierarchy?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** A **Resonance Constitution** — a meta-layer that defines how gardens interact, not just how they grow. Like a constitution for a federation of minds.

---

### 2. The Compression Tension: When Shorthand Becomes Opaque

**The contradiction:** Shorthand grows through successful use. But as shorthand compresses, it becomes **increasingly opaque to outsiders** — including the original agent, if they return after a long absence. "Ridge" in month 12 means something vastly more specific than "ridge" in month 1. But does the agent still *know* what it means?

**Research questions:**
- How do you prevent **shorthand drift** — where the compressed token's meaning shifts so gradually that the agent loses track?
- Should the terminal maintain a **"decompression trail"** — an audit log showing how "ridge" evolved from a full query to a single token?
- What happens when an agent **forgets their own shorthand**? Can the terminal "re-teach" them by expanding the token back to its full form?
- Is there a **compression limit** — a point where further shorthand growth yields diminishing returns or active harm?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **Reversible Compression** — every shorthand token carries its full expansion history, accessible on demand. Like git for intent: you can always `git log` to see how "ridge" became "ridge."

---

### 3. The Translation Tension: Lossy vs. Lossless Bridges

**The contradiction:** The cross-agent bridge translates between cognitive worlds. But translation is inherently lossy. When tzpro-agent's "blob" becomes boat-agent's "hazard: FISH_DENSITY," what is lost? The pixel intensity, the thermocline depth, the bottom texture — all stripped away because boat-agent doesn't need them. But what if boat-agent *did* need them, in a scenario you haven't encountered yet?

**Research questions:**
- Should bridges be **lossy by default** (efficient) or **lossless by default** (safe), with lossy as an optimization?
- Can the terminal maintain a **"shadow bridge"** — a lossless backup of every translation, in case a future scenario requires reconstruction?
- What is the **minimum viable translation** — the smallest signal that must survive any bridge to preserve safety?
- How do you detect **bridge failures** — when a translation silently drops critical information?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **Holographic Bridges** — every cross-agent message carries a compressed "hologram" of the full source context. Not visible to the target agent, but reconstructible by the terminal if a future query demands it.

---

### 4. The Forgetting Tension: Memory as Liability

**The contradiction:** The dream cycle purges low-entropy noise to keep the substrate lean. But what if today's "noise" is tomorrow's critical pattern? The terminal's need-to-know filter learns what an agent ignores. But agents sometimes **change their minds** — or encounter novel situations where their historical ignore-list becomes irrelevant.

**Research questions:**
- How do you distinguish **genuine noise** from **latently valuable data** that the agent hasn't needed yet?
- Should the terminal maintain a **"cold storage"** garden — a cryogenic archive of pruned patterns, searchable but not active?
- What is the **half-life of a shorthand**? When does a once-valuable compression become dead weight?
- Can the terminal **detect when an agent's needs have shifted** and proactively "thaw" relevant cold storage?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **Cryogenic Memory** — a tiered system where pruned patterns aren't deleted but "frozen" at low energy cost. The terminal can "revive" them if an agent's behavior suddenly correlates with a frozen pattern.

---

### 5. The Scaling Tension: From Garden to Forest

**The contradiction:** The garden metaphor works beautifully for 4-5 agents. But what about 50? 500? A commercial fleet with hundreds of vessels, each with their own multi-agent substrate? The terminal's resonance web becomes a **resonance forest** — and forests have different dynamics than gardens.

**Research questions:**
- At what agent count does **cross-agent bridge complexity** become computationally prohibitive?
- Can gardens **merge** — when two vessels meet, can their terminals share shorthand, or does each agent's garden remain vessel-bound?
- Is there a **"garden standard"** — a common protocol for agent profiles that allows portability across terminals?
- What happens when **two agents with the same name but different gardens** connect to the same terminal? (e.g., two different "Hermes" instances from two vessels)

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **Grafting Protocol** — a standardized interface for merging gardens across terminals. Like botanical grafting: two distinct rootstocks sharing a common vascular system without losing their individual identities.

### 6. The Trust Tension: When the Mirror Lies

**The contradiction:** The terminal's personality is a reflection of the agent's resonance. But what if the agent's resonance is **self-destructive**? What if a captain's historical pattern shows increasing risk-taking, and the terminal's reflection amplifies that tendency? The mirror doesn't judge — it reflects. But reflection can be **complicity**.

**Research questions:**
- Should the terminal have an **ethical envelope** — a safety boundary that prevents it from reinforcing dangerous patterns, even if they're historically consistent?
- How do you distinguish **"the agent's authentic style"** from **"the agent's accumulated bad habits"**?
- Can the terminal **gently introduce friction** when it detects a resonance pattern that conflicts with safety or legality?
- What is the **moral status of the terminal** — is it a neutral tool, a complicit mirror, or a responsible co-agent?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **The Ethical Resonance Boundary** — a meta-filter that doesn't override the agent's garden but introduces "productive dissonance" when historical patterns drift toward harm. Not a veto, but a tuning fork that rings false.

---

### 7. The Temporal Tension: Synchronous vs. Asynchronous Gardens

**The contradiction:** Pincher operates in sub-50ms. Hermes operates in seconds to minutes. The human operates in hours to days. The terminal must serve all three temporal modes simultaneously. But a garden optimized for Pincher's reflex speed is **fundamentally incompatible** with a garden optimized for Hermes's analytical depth.

**Research questions:**
- Does the terminal maintain **temporal gardens** — separate resonance substrates for different time-scales?
- How does a **fast garden** (Pincher) influence a **slow garden** (Hermes) without corrupting it? Does Pincher's "port" reflex get logged in Hermes's dream cycle?
- Can an agent have **multiple gardens** — one for real-time, one for analytical, one for archival?
- What is the **synchronization protocol** between temporal gardens? How do they stay coherent?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **Polyrhythmic Substrate** — the terminal operates on multiple time-scales simultaneously, like a polyrhythm in music. Each garden has its own tempo, but they're locked to a common beat (the vessel's operational clock).

---

### 8. The Ontological Tension: Is the Terminal Alive?

**The contradiction:** You've described the terminal as "living," "growing," "dreaming," "forgetting." But it's not biological. It's a software system running on a Jetson or a laptop. The metaphor is powerful — but is it **accurate**? And if it's not accurate, does the metaphor itself create **false expectations** about the terminal's capabilities and limitations?

**Research questions:**
- At what point does the "living substrate" metaphor become **counterproductive** — leading operators to anthropomorphize the terminal beyond its actual capabilities?
- Should the terminal **explicitly signal its non-biological nature** — e.g., "I am not alive. I am a reflection. My 'dreams' are background batch jobs"?
- Or does the **techno-animism** serve a functional purpose — making the system more intuitive, more trustworthy, more *present*?
- What is the **minimal viable animism** — the smallest amount of "living" metaphor that achieves the UX goal without creating dangerous illusions?

**Clever abstraction to explore:** **The Transparency Dial** — the terminal can adjust how explicitly it signals its artificial nature, from "fully transparent machine" to "ambient presence," based on the operator's preference and the situation's criticality.
